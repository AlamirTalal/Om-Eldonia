The importance of education

Education is one of the sources of investment in human capital and represents the path of development, progress and transit to eradicate poverty and build people, and this is what was put forward in Egypt's vision for sustainable development 2030

First, pre-university education

In August 2014, specialized councils were formed to exchange ideas in the development of education and scientific research, and in December 2014 Egypt's Vision 2030 was launched to build a person capable of learning and innovation

The Ministry of Education and Technical Education has taken serious steps to raise the quality of education in Egypt, including:

1- Transforming the education system from qualifying the student to take the exam to a "way of life" that works on developing the student's creative abilities and critical thinking 2- Building a modern education system based on the latest international standards (from kindergarten to the fifth grade of primary school.) 3- Developing learning and assessment at the secondary stage in order to ensure that students achieve real learning outcomes. 4- Establishing question banks for the secondary stage in cooperation with the National Center for Examinations and Educational Evaluation 5- Building 75,000 new classrooms to solve the crisis of classroom densities at a cost of 24 billion pounds 6- December 2015 negotiations were held with international knowledge institutions and the technological infrastructure of the Egyptian Knowledge Bank, and in January 2016 the Egyptian Knowledge Bank was launched www.ekb.eg, which is the largest digital library and an electronic knowledge center in the world, which provides free access to educational and scientific publications in many /p>

Developments

New Education System

In April 2016, the idea of ​​the new Egyptian education (Education 2.0) was launched at the World Bank conference. Its themes were (the new education system - Egyptian-Japanese schools - attention to people with special needs - the modified assessment system in the secondary stage - a teacher Applied technology - building and renovating educational buildings to solve the problem of densities) and implementation has been started since 2017

Curriculum development

A general framework for the curricula has been established on the basis of which the new books of the new education system that is now in force from kindergarten to grade 5 of primary school were built. The Minister of Education explained that the books of the new education system focus on the learner himself. So that these books encourage the student to the idea of ​​learning for life and not for the exam, where the importance of the idea of ​​lifelong learning based on competency and revealing the real talents of the student is established, pointing out that these books offer interdisciplinary learning

Government International Schools

13 distinguished international government schools have been established for middle-income, and these schools work to teach the curriculum in the British system, whose philosophy is based on providing a distinguished educational service at an appropriate cost to a segment of the Egyptian society, with the technical support of the International School Owners Foundation.

Egyptian-Japanese Schools Project

In 2018, the Egyptian-Japanese Schools project was established in cooperation with the Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA), and these schools implement the new education system curricula in addition to Tokatsu activities, where 35 Japanese schools were operated in 2018/ 2019 in 21 governorates, then 6 schools were operated in 2019/2020, then two schools will be added in the 2020/2021 academic year, and there are 9 new schools in 20201/2022. Missions of teachers were sent to receive training in Japan, where 800 teachers were trained

Enable digital learning

Egypt has invested in digital learning from 2017, by injecting additional curricula from Britannica, Pearson and Nahdet Misr in Arabic and English into the Egyptian Knowledge Bank, and began providing multiple learning resources and making digital content available to develop self-learning skill. 25,000 technology specialists were trained to provide technical support to students Infrastructure has been built in all secondary schools, which are: Providing 1.8 million tablets to students, installing 36,000 smart screens in classrooms, and connecting Viber networks to 2425 schools - The learning management platform was built from the Egyptian Knowledge Bank for secondary school students, and interactive educational content linked to the curriculum was made available to all secondary education students. - The electronic examination system has been implemented, after question banks have been prepared from the National Examination Center (to examine at least one million and 800 thousand students simultaneously).

Attention to improving education for people with special needs

-In 2017/2018, procedures to facilitate the integration of students with mild disabilities in schools, the preparation of classes for multi-disabilities, and the training of teachers of this category began. - In 2019/2020: a framework for intellectual education curricula was developed, and new learning outcomes for the blind were identified

Establishing applied technology schools for technical education students

Establishing a body to ensure the quality of technical education

In 2018: The President of the Republic agreed with Germany to establish a body to ensure the quality and accreditation of educational, training, technical, technical and vocational institutions and programmes. This body will help ensure independence, transparency, and provide the distinguished skills required by employers in the local and international market

Second: University Higher Education

He witnessed many educational achievements and projects achieved during the era of President Abdel Fattah Al-Sisi. The achievements included a remarkable development in all sectors of higher education. The ministry has made great strides in providing higher education services, which aims to improve higher education and develop its systems, programs and work mechanisms.

To keep pace with the requirements of the state's plan for sustainable development 2030, such as:

1- The increase in the number of universities with advanced positions in international rankings 2- Increasing the number of universities to 27 public and 29 private 3- Implementation of 34 national projects, including 8 private international universities, and they are: • 8 private international universities, namely: (Galala University, Higher Academy of Sciences, New Alamein University, New Mansoura University, King Salman University, Egyptian-Japanese University of Science and Technology, Zewail City of Science and Technology, rehabilitation of the National French University in Egypt) • (4) new public universities: Matrouh, New Valley, Luxor, Hurghada • (3) Universities of Technology in New Cairo, Quesna, and Beni Suef • (3) research institutes and centers, namely: (Egyptian Space City, Electronics Research Institute, National Institute for Astronomical and Geophysical Research) • (8) branches of international universities, namely: (Canadian Universities Complex, International Knowledge Universities, Global University, German International University, European Universities Complex, Capital University, International Academic Complex, Egyptian American University for Medical Sciences) • (7) Technology parks, and the House of Egypt in Paris for cultural representation, as well as ongoing projects in universities and other research centers.
